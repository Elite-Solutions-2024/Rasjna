Chapati

Rolling into the week with some roti love. Who’s with me?
Flour, water, love – the ingredients for a perfect roti.

Deliciousness served on a plate.

Ingredients:

- 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta) - Water, as needed - Salt, to taste - 1-2 tablespoons oil or ghee (clarified butter), for kneading and cooking

Instructions:

1. *Prepare the Dough:* - In a large mixing bowl, add the whole wheat flour and salt. Mix well. - Gradually add water to the flour while kneading to form a soft, smooth, and pliable dough. The amount of water needed may vary, so add it slowly until the dough comes together. - Once the dough comes together, knead it well for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. - Rub a few drops of oil or ghee over the surface of the dough to prevent it from drying out. - Cover the dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes. This resting period helps to relax the gluten and makes the dough easier to roll out.

2. *Divide and Shape the Dough:* - After resting, divide the dough into equal-sized portions, roughly the size of a golf ball. - Roll each portion between your palms to form a smooth ball. - Flatten each ball slightly with your palm to make it easier to roll out.

3. *Roll out the Chapatis:* - Heat a tawa (griddle) or a flat-bottomed skillet over medium-high heat. - Take one portion of the dough and dust it lightly with flour. - Using a rolling pin, roll out the dough into a thin, round disc of about 6-8 inches in diameter. Roll it evenly to maintain a uniform thickness. - If the dough sticks to the rolling pin or the rolling surface, dust it with a little more flour as needed.

4. *Cook the Chapatis:* - Once the tawa is hot, place the rolled-out chapati onto the tawa. - Cook the chapati for about 30 seconds to 1 minute on one side until small bubbles start to appear on the surface. - Flip the chapati using a spatula. - Cook the other side for another 30 seconds to 1 minute until light brown spots appear and the chapati is fully cooked. - If necessary, lightly press the edges of the chapati with a clean kitchen towel or spatula to help it puff up.

5. *Apply Ghee (Optional):* - Optionally, you can brush the cooked chapati with a little oil or ghee for extra flavor and softness.

6. *Repeat the Process:* - Repeat the rolling and cooking process with the remaining dough portions to make more chapatis.

7. *Keep Warm and Serve:* - As each chapati is cooked, stack them on a plate and cover them with a clean kitchen towel to keep them warm and soft. - Serve the chapatis hot with your favorite curry, dal, or any side dish of your choice.

Enjoy your freshly made chapatis!
