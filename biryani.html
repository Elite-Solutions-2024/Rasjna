Biryani

Spice up your feed with biryani.
Why is biryani so emotional to all? Biryani holds a special place in the hearts of many because it's more than just a dish; it's a culinary masterpiece steeped in history, culture, and tradition.

Deliciousness served on a plate.

Ingredients:

- 500 grams chicken, preferably bone-in pieces - 2 cups basmati rice - 2 onions, thinly sliced - 2 tomatoes, chopped - 1/2 cup plain yogurt - 4 cloves garlic, minced - 1-inch piece of ginger, minced - 2 green chilies, slit - 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves (cilantro) - 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves - 2 tablespoons biryani masala - 1 teaspoon turmeric powder - 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste) - 1/2 teaspoon garam masala - 1/4 cup cooking oil or ghee - Salt to taste - Water as needed - Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (optional) - Fried onions for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
2. In a bowl, marinate the chicken with yogurt, ginger, garlic, green chilies, half of the chopped coriander leaves, half of the chopped mint leaves, biryani masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes or longer in the refrigerator.
3. In a large pot or deep skillet, heat the cooking oil or ghee over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and fry until they turn golden brown. Remove half of the fried onions and set aside for garnish.
4. Add the marinated chicken to the pot and cook until the chicken is partially cooked, stirring occasionally.
5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft and mushy.
6. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pot. Stir gently to combine with the chicken and spices.
7. Add enough water to just cover the rice and chicken. Season with salt if needed. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer until the rice is cooked and the chicken is tender, about 15-20 minutes.
8. Once the rice is cooked, fluff it gently with a fork. If using saffron, pour the saffron milk over the rice.
9. Garnish the biryani with the remaining chopped coriander leaves, mint leaves, and fried onions.
10. Serve the chicken biryani hot with raita (yogurt sauce) or salad on the side.
Adjust the spice levels according to your taste preference. Enjoy your homemade chicken biryani!
